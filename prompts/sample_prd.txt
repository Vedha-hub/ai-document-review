# Product Requirements Document: CampusRide

## 1. Executive Summary
CampusRide is a mobile-first ride-sharing application designed exclusively for university and college ecosystems. Unlike commercial incumbents like Uber or Lyft, CampusRide fosters a community-centric model that prioritizes verified student safety, cost-effective micro-transfers, and social connection. The platform enables students to share rides to local off-campus apartments, regional transit hubs, or weekend excursions. By leveraging ".edu" email verification, we create a "trusted network" environment that mitigates the risks associated with ride-sharing with strangers. 

The value proposition is two-fold: it provides an affordable transportation solution for students living on tight budgets and reduces the carbon footprint of the campus by encouraging carpooling. We will launch initially as a pilot at three major universities with high commuter populations. Our business model focuses on a low-transaction-fee structure and, eventually, campus-integrated partnerships for university events. By positioning CampusRide as the "official" campus transit partner, we solve the lingering mobility gap that occurs during late-night hours when campus shuttles are unavailable, while simultaneously fostering a sense of shared community among the student body.

## 2. Problem Statement
The modern college campus faces a significant "last-mile" transportation crisis. Public transit is often unreliable, campus shuttles stop running before library hours conclude, and private ride-sharing services have become prohibitively expensive for students due to surge pricing and high booking fees. Furthermore, for many students, the lack of a vehicle creates a social barrier, limiting their ability to visit off-campus housing or engage in regional internships. 

Existing solutions fail to account for the unique safety requirements of the student body. Women and minority students often express anxiety when getting into vehicles with unknown civilian drivers. Additionally, there is a lack of trust in the vetting processes of commercial apps. Because there is currently no dedicated marketplace for student-to-student rides, many students resort to informal, unverified coordination via group chats (e.g., WhatsApp or GroupMe), which lack safety tracking, payment integration, and liability protections. This creates a fragmented, risky, and inefficient environment where students are either stranded, paying exorbitant prices, or participating in unsafe, undocumented transportation arrangements. CampusRide addresses this by professionalizing the student-peer commute.

## 3. User Personas

**Persona 1: The Commuter Student**
*   **Name:** Marcus, 21
*   **Occupation:** Junior, Engineering Major
*   **Goals:** Save money on daily travel from his apartment to the Engineering building; avoid parking permit costs.
*   **Frustrations:** Campus bus schedules don't align with his lab hours; commercial ride apps cost $15–$20 per trip, which he cannot afford daily.

**Persona 2: The Social Planner**
*   **Name:** Sarah, 19
*   **Occupation:** Sophomore, Liberal Arts
*   **Goals:** Get to weekend concerts or regional shopping centers with friends safely.
*   **Frustrations:** Fear of riding with unknown adult drivers; social anxiety about splitting costs/reimbursement among friend groups.

## 4. User Stories
1. **As a student**, I want to verify my account with my university email so that I am only riding with other verified members of my campus.
2. **As a student driver**, I want to post my route in advance so that I can find passengers heading in the same direction.
3. **As a rider**, I want to see the driver’s profile and mutual friends so that I feel comfortable getting into their vehicle.
4. **As a user**, I want to split fares automatically within the app so that I don't have to chase people for gas money.
5. **As a user**, I want a "Share My Ride" safety button so that my roommates can track my GPS location during the trip.

## 5. Functional Requirements
*   **FR-1:** App must require a valid .edu email domain to sign up and participate.
*   **FR-2:** The system must include a driver-rating and passenger-rating feature after each completed ride.
*   **FR-3:** App must allow drivers to set a recurring route (e.g., Daily commute from Apartment A to Campus B).
*   **FR-4:** System must provide real-time GPS tracking shared with a pre-selected "Safety Contact."
*   **FR-5:** The platform must integrate a payment gateway (Stripe) to handle in-app fare splitting.
*   **FR-6:** App must have an "In-App Chat" feature that masks personal phone numbers for privacy.
*   **FR-7:** The system shall display the driver’s car make, model, and license plate number upon ride acceptance.
*   **FR-8:** App must allow administrators to flag and ban users who violate community safety guidelines.

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements
*   **Performance:** The app must load ride availability in under 2 seconds.
*   **Security:** All user data (including email and payment info) must be encrypted using AES-256 standards.
*   **Scalability:** The backend infrastructure must support up to 10,000 concurrent users per campus during peak hours (e.g., Friday nights).
*   **Availability:** The system must maintain 99.9% uptime to ensure reliability during critical transit times.

## 7. Success Metrics / KPIs
1. **Daily Active Users (DAU):** Achieve 500 active users per pilot campus within the first 30 days.
2. **Ride Completion Rate:** Maintain a 95% completion rate (rides requested vs. rides successfully finished).
3. **Safety Incidents:** Target 0 reported physical safety incidents per 10,000 rides.
4. **User Acquisition Cost (UAC):** Keep acquisition cost below $2.00 per user through university marketing channels.

## 8. Edge Cases and Risk Analysis
1. **Risk:** Students using the app for non-campus purposes. *Mitigation:* Implement geofencing to limit start/end points to the campus vicinity.
2. **Risk:** Driver doesn't show up. *Mitigation:* Implement a "No-Show" reporting tool that penalizes driver ratings.
3. **Risk:** Misuse of the chat function. *Mitigation:* Automated AI-based keyword filtering to block harassment/inappropriate language.
4. **Risk:** Liability regarding student accidents. *Mitigation:* Mandatory Terms of Service agreement stating the app acts as a connector, not a transit employer.

## 9. Technical Specifications
*   **Tech Stack:** React Native (Frontend), Node.js/Express (Backend), PostgreSQL (Database).
*   **Architecture:** Microservices architecture to isolate ride-matching, user profiles, and payment processing.
*   **APIs:** Google Maps API for geolocation/routing; Stripe API for payments; Twilio API for SMS alerts.
*   **Database:** AWS RDS (PostgreSQL) for relational data, Redis for caching active ride sessions.
