# Product Requirements Document: CampusRide

## 1. Executive Summary
CampusRide is a specialized, hyper-local ride-sharing platform designed exclusively for college and university environments. Unlike generic, high-cost ride-hailing services (e.g., Uber or Lyft) that prioritize professional drivers, CampusRide facilitates peer-to-peer transportation, allowing students with cars to offer rides to fellow students needing transit to classes, off-campus housing, or local transit hubs. 

The core value proposition is affordability, safety, and community building. By leveraging university-issued email addresses (e.g., .edu domains), the platform creates a verified "walled garden" that fosters trust among users. CampusRide addresses the specific pain points of the student demographic, such as limited budget, lack of parking on campus, and the need for reliable transport during late-night study sessions or weekend events. The app will feature real-time ride tracking, integrated cashless payments, and social verification badges. This product aims to reduce the carbon footprint of campus traffic, alleviate parking shortages, and provide a convenient mobility solution that integrates seamlessly into the academic lifestyle.

## 2. Problem Statement
The modern college campus is plagued by three primary issues: parking scarcity, high transportation costs, and limited mobility. Many students living off-campus struggle with the unreliability of public transit systems, which often fail to align with erratic class schedules or late-night extracurricular obligations. Conversely, commercial ride-sharing services are prohibitively expensive for students on fixed budgets, often charging surge pricing that makes daily commuting unsustainable. 

Furthermore, current ride-sharing platforms offer no sense of community or vetting relevant to the student experience. Students are often wary of getting into cars with complete strangers. There is a clear market gap for a secure, community-driven platform where the driver and passenger are connected through a shared academic identity. Without this, students continue to rely on fragmented social media groups (like Facebook "Ride Board" pages) which lack real-time tracking, secure payment gateways, and safety accountability. This inefficiency leads to missed classes, isolation for students living in remote housing, and a massive waste of resources. CampusRide exists to formalize this ecosystem, providing a safe, affordable, and technologically modern bridge for student mobility.

## 3. User Personas

**Persona 1: The Commuter Student**
*   **Name:** Sarah Jenkins
*   **Age:** 20
*   **Occupation:** Junior, Psychology Major
*   **Goals:** Get to her 8:00 AM lectures on time without paying $20+ for a commercial ride or searching for parking for 30 minutes.
*   **Frustrations:** Public buses are infrequent and unreliable; she hates arriving at class stressed and late.

**Persona 2: The Campus Driver**
*   **Name:** Marcus Thorne
*   **Age:** 22
*   **Occupation:** Senior, Engineering Major
*   **Goals:** Offset the cost of gas and car maintenance by picking up students along his usual commute route.
*   **Frustrations:** Driving alone feels wasteful, and he finds the parking permit fees for his off-campus apartment to be a major financial drain.

## 4. User Stories
1. **As a** student rider, **I want** to filter potential drivers by their verified university department **so that** I can ride with someone I share common ground with.
2. **As a** student driver, **I want** to pre-set my daily commute route **so that** the app can automatically match me with riders heading in my direction.
3. **As a** student rider, **I want** to see a safety badge on a driver's profile **so that** I feel confident about my physical safety during the ride.
4. **As a** student, **I want** to pay via an in-app wallet **so that** I don't have to carry cash or handle complex split payments.
5. **As a** user, **I want** to send an automatic "I'm on my way" alert with live tracking to a trusted contact **so that** my friends know I am safe.

## 5. Functional Requirements
*   **FR-1:** The app must verify user identity via a valid .edu email address before allowing ride requests.
*   **FR-2:** The app shall allow drivers to set static recurring routes (e.g., Mon-Fri, 7:30 AM).
*   **FR-3:** The system must implement a real-time GPS tracking interface for active rides.
*   **FR-4:** The payment engine must support peer-to-peer splitting of ride costs to keep trips affordable.
*   **FR-5:** The platform shall include a two-way rating system (1–5 stars) and a reporting feature for inappropriate behavior.
*   **FR-6:** The app must provide an "SOS/Emergency" button that shares location with campus security.
*   **FR-7:** The system shall allow riders to request a ride for a future time slot, not just on-demand.
*   **FR-8:** The platform shall implement a push notification service for ride status updates (Driver arriving, Rider picked up, Trip complete).

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements
*   **Performance:** The app must load ride search results in under 2 seconds.
*   **Security:** All user data (including PII and location history) must be encrypted using AES-256; authentication must require MFA.
*   **Scalability:** The backend must support concurrent requests for up to 50,000 active students per campus.
*   **Availability:** The service must maintain 99.9% uptime during peak campus hours (7 AM – 9 PM).

## 7. Success Metrics / KPIs
*   **Adoption Rate:** Achieve 25% of the total student population registered as active users within the first 6 months.
*   **Match Efficiency:** Ensure 70% of requested rides find a driver match within 10 minutes.
*   **Safety Rating:** Maintain an average user safety rating of 4.8/5.0 across all rides.
*   **Cost Savings:** Demonstrate an average cost reduction of 60% compared to commercial ride-hailing services for the same route.

## 8. Edge Cases and Risk Analysis
*   **Risk 1:** A driver or rider acts inappropriately. *Mitigation:* Zero-tolerance policy, permanent bans, and an immediate reporting loop to campus safety.
*   **Risk 2:** High traffic density during finals week causing server crashes. *Mitigation:* Implement auto-scaling clusters on cloud infrastructure (e.g., AWS EKS).
*   **Risk 3:** Users circumventing the app to pay outside the platform. *Mitigation:* Gamify the "In-App" experience with rewards and loyalty points for using official payment channels.
*   **Risk 4:** Insurance liability issues in the event of an accident. *Mitigation:* Require all drivers to acknowledge legal disclaimers and recommend supplemental ride-sharing insurance coverage.

## 9. Technical Specifications
*   **Tech Stack:** React Native (Frontend), Node.js/Express (Backend), PostgreSQL (Relational Data), Redis (Caching/Real-time GPS).
*   **Architecture:** Microservices-based architecture to handle independent scaling of the Authentication, Matching, and Payment services.
*   **APIs:** Google Maps API (Directions/Geocoding), Stripe API (Payments), Twilio API (SMS verification/Notifications).
*   **Database:** PostgreSQL for persistent user and ride history; MongoDB for unstructured chat logs and driver route preferences.
